## Faster Transit for Small Hulls

Helios Drift Systems builds propulsion for ships that do real work: survey boats, rescue cutters, medical shuttles, and owner-operated couriers. Our new **M1 Hyperdrive** reduces jump prep friction and gives light craft a practical inter-system range profile without turning the vessel into a maintenance nightmare.

The M1 is built for captains who fly frequently, dock often, and cannot afford week-long downtime between missions. It favors reliability, serviceability, and predictable thermal behavior over fragile peak numbers.

## What Is New in M1

- **Micro-singularity injector redesign**: Faster charge stabilization, fewer abort cycles during hot restarts.
- **Adaptive field geometry**: Better jump envelope control for light asymmetrical hulls.
- **Service-first layout**: All high-wear assemblies are front-access, tool-light, and labeled for one-technician swaps.
- **Quiet thermal profile**: Lower acoustic and heat signatures in station-adjacent operations.

## Political and Market Context

Regional traffic authorities are tightening small-craft safety standards while frontier contracts are expanding. That combination favors propulsion systems that are both fast and auditable. The M1 was designed for this moment: measurable compliance behavior, stable software governance, and hardware that can be inspected quickly by third-party certifiers.

On the market side, independent operators are no longer competing only on speed. They compete on **schedule confidence**. A drive that jumps slightly slower but misses fewer windows is economically superior over a quarter.

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Medical transfer fleets** that need predictable departure windows.
- **Disaster-response tenders** that jump into unstable logistics zones.
- **Scientific survey groups** moving between short-notice observation points.
- **Independent freight couriers** running tight margin routes.

## Why It Matters

Small starships are usually treated as scaled-down versions of large freighters. They are not. They are different operational animals: tighter crews, shorter maintenance windows, and missions that change hour to hour. The M1 treats that reality as a design constraint from day one.
