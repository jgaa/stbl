---
title: I Like Cakes
published: 2025-01-18 10:20
tags: food, baking, personal, weekend
---

I like cakes because they turn a normal afternoon into an occasion. Even a plain vanilla sponge feels special when the kitchen smells warm and sweet.

My first attempts were rough. I overmixed batter, opened the oven too early, and once used salt instead of sugar in the frosting. The texture was wrong, the shape collapsed, and the result still disappeared because everyone laughed and ate it anyway.

After a few weekends I learned to slow down and treat each step as separate work. Measure first, mix second, bake without interruptions, then cool fully before touching frosting. That one change improved almost everything.

Now I keep a small notebook with versions of the same base recipe. One page tracks cocoa amount, another tracks bake time for different pans, and another tracks frosting sweetness. Tiny adjustments matter more than dramatic changes.

Cakes are also practical social glue. If I bring a simple sheet cake to a meetup, people stay longer and conversation starts more easily. Food gives everyone something shared before the real topics begin.

My current favorite is lemon cake with a light glaze. It is bright, not too heavy, and easy to slice for a crowd. It also works well with coffee, which means I rarely have leftovers.

The long-term goal is to become consistent, not perfect. I want the fiftieth cake to be reliably good on a tired Friday night, not just great on a lucky Sunday.

Baking has become my reset button. It is hands-on, predictable, and rewarding in a way that screens are not.
